      The reduced vowel or schwa: Ə is the most common sound in English. The contrast between stressed vowels and reduced vowels creates a characteristic rhythm in spoken English.

      Role of vowel reduction in telephone communication: second language learners must notice the difference between long, stressed vowels and short unstressed vowels.

      This contrast creates English stress and rhythm.

      Often, structure words such as articles (a:Ə, the: thƏ), auxiliary verbs

      (can: cƏn, was: wƏs), prepositions (for: fƏr, of: Əf) and pronouns (your: yƏr, them: thƏm) are reduced (schwa sound):

      Examples of schwa:

      Schwa reduction in words with r: flowƏr (flower), sistƏr (sister), colƏr (colour)

  1. Module 6
    • Listening Techniques - Focus Words

      Focus is defined as a central point of attraction, attention, or activity. The focus word gets extra emphasis so that the listener can hear it clearly.

      Related: Basic Emphasis Pattern where content words are emphasized and structure words are usually de-emphasized

      Example: Get me the book. Book is the focus word

      For focus words the speaker’s pitch changes. The voice rises then falls. The contrast i.e. rises and falls make the focus word stand out.

      When a conversation starts, focus word is usually the last content word. However, after a conversation starts (i.e the person who replies/follows up), any word can be a focus word (not only the last content word) - the emotion might dictate the focus word.

    • Listening Techniques - Reduction

      Reduction, which features eliminating sounds and de-emphasizing vowels, is a normal part of everyday speech

      hearing the reductions in native speech is an important skill to ensure effective communication.

      What is Reduction de-emphasized,
      left out sounds,
      Contraction of a verb omits a sound,
      Blending also omits a sound,
      omitting a sound when adding to any part of speech

      Examples of Reduction Vowels that are de-emphasized,
      sounds that are left out
      and other changes of the complete word form such as

      havta instead of have to;
      usta instead of used to.

      Contraction of a verb omits a sound: it is becomes it’s, does not becomes doesn’t, are becomes ‘re as in we’re, you’re.

      Blending also omits a sound but it is a less formal construction, omitting a sound when adding to any part of speech: a noun:
      Alice’s for Alice is/has;
      a pronoun/adjective/adverb: this’s for this is, or an adverb/pronoun/noun/adjective:
      there’re for there are

      native speakers employ reduction while speaking in every environment: so try your best to understand reduction

    • Pitch Direction and Intonation – Your Telephone Voice

      Using the correct pitch direction when you speak gives your listener all the cues needed for a smooth exchange of information.
      Pitch direction becomes even more important when it comes to telephone conversations.

      Role of voice pitch and intonation in effective oral communication Pitch is how high or low your voice goes. Pitch direction is whether your voice goes up or down. Intonation on the other hand is all the different pitches (rising and falling tones) you use within a word, phrase or sentence. One of the most commonly known roles of intonation in the English language is to transforming a statement into a question,

      every sentence has at least one pitch word

      Pitch words: that the speaker chooses to convey the message of the statement or question. the rising or the lowering of the voice helps to identify the Pitch words

      The easiest way to identify a high pitch word is to listen for the highness of the stressed syllable on that word. A high pitch word is mostly used to indicate the listener that the word is the focus point of the conversation
